$25K Electric Pickup Unveiled by Slate Auto

Slate Auto, a new EV startup backed by Jeff Bezos, has made waves by unveiling the "Blank Slate" — a compact electric pickup priced at just $25,000 Designed for practicality and personalization, the Blank Slate offers a base driving range of 150 miles, with an optional upgrade to 240 miles for longer trips. With its minimalist design, modular add-ons, and customizable features, Slate Auto targets drivers who need a versatile, affordable EV without sacrificing style. The company also promises a wide selection of over 100 accessories, making the Blank Slate a highly adaptable choice for work or adventure. As major automakers race to dominate the electric truck market, Slate Auto’s bold pricing and focus on customization could make it a serious disruptor in the EV world.

Trump Considers Tariff Relief for Automakers

Former President Donald Trump is reportedly considering tariff exemptions for automakers as the U.S. prepares to impose new 25% tariffs on Chinese auto parts in early May. The proposed tariffs have raised concerns across the automotive industry, with manufacturers warning of potential production disruptions and increased vehicle prices.In response, Trump’s team is exploring targeted relief options to shield automakers from the financial strain. The goal is to support domestic production and avoid passing costs onto consumers. Industry leaders have cautiously welcomed the news, saying relief could provide short-term stability amid ongoing global trade tensions. While no official decisions have been announced, the move could play a significant role in shaping the economic outlook for U.S. automakers heading into the second half of 2025.

Porsche Faces Sales Challenges in China

Porsche is facing increased pressure in the Chinese automotive market, as local electric vehicle brands gain momentum. Despite unveiling new models at the Shanghai Auto Show, the luxury automaker is struggling to maintain its sales dominance in a market that is rapidly shifting toward affordable, high-performance EVs. Brands like Xiaomi, with offerings such as the SU7 Ultra, are delivering cutting-edge technology and strong performance at lower price points, challenging the traditional appeal of foreign luxury cars. Porsche remains committed to the Chinese market, but analysts suggest the brand may need to adapt more aggressively to local tastes and the rising demand for premium electric options. As domestic Chinese automakers continue to rise, even globally respected brands like Porsche must rethink strategies to stay competitive in the world’s largest auto market.
